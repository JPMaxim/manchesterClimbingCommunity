Sustainability in Climbing

What are the ways in which we can increase our sustainability as climbers? This page explores some of the ways in which you can reduce the impact that your passion for climbing places on the environment.

How can we can be more sustainable outdoors?

  • Leave No Trace - Following Leave No Trace principles is essential in minimising our impact on the environment. It's as simple as taking everything you brought (including rubbish) away with you, sticking to established footpaths, and respecting wildlife.

  • Gear From Recycled Materials - When it comes to gear, we can make a conscious choice to support eco-friendly options. Look for equipment made from recycled materials or can be recycled or repurposed at the end of its lifespan.

  • Conservation - Supporting organisations that work to protect climbing areas and the environment is a powerful way to make a difference. Research the local charities in your favourite climbing area and donate what you can or volunteer your time to participate in conservation efforts.

  • Transport - When travelling to the crag or to the gym, consider using public transport, carpooling or, where possible, biking. All these methods can reduce our emissions and minimise our environmental impact when compared to driving.

How can we can be more sustainable indoors?

  • Facilities - Reusing and re-purposing buildings that would otherwise fall into disrepair, is a great way that climbing gyms can reduce the impact on the environment of building and opening new climbing gyms. Gyms can be mindful of the use of materials such as foam (boulder matting), rubber (rubber crumb floors) and plastic (holds) that are so crucial to our experience but have a high toxicity factor at production.

  • Consumables - The clothing we wear when climbing contributes to the clothing industry's extremely harmful effect on the environment. Patagonia, Prana, 3rd Rock are a sample of brands whose identity revolves around sustainability and positive impacts and you can further reduce your impact by buying second-hand.
